Planning

At WDDS, the design process begins by meeting one-on-one with our clients. This first meeting is no charge. Understanding you means understanding your needs. We will research your industry and how you have used digital products in the past. Once we have answers to the critical questions that arise, we will begin to plan the most essential steps in creating a design that communicates the message, makes your product useful, and is aesthetically refined.

Content Creation

After our initial meeting, we immediately begin the process of drafting your web content. Your new digital content [engaging text, photography, and video] will be dynamic, interactive and fully responsive. From pre-production through post-production, I will work closely with you and my partners to ensure a seamless process with expert execution.

Web Design

The standards, trends, and techniques in web design are constantly evolving. The solution to adapting in this environment requires following three principles:
1. Keep it simple.
2. Engaging, and
3. Interactive.

Development

Once you've approved the initial design and we all understand the project goals, I will begin to execute the plans we've agreed on. Every bit of craftsmanship will be brought ot the table to shape your new digital identity. I will always insist that our products be launched in strategic increments so we cal begin to collect real user feedback at an early stage.

Testing/ Launch

There is always one daunting question that needs to be answered head on - is this working? We attack that fear, with confidence and planning, through our launching process. Constant evaluation of our products, using analytics, gives us the flexibility to to adjust tactics quickly in order to acheive the desired results.

Support

A better course website

St. Benedict's Prep Algebra

What can be done to make course material more accessible to the post-mellinial generation? I may have had an iPod, a cellphone, a computer, and internet access in my home as a child, but all of those technological advances in the mid to late 90s pale in comparison to the processing power and speed of today's technology. All of which, amazingly, my students have possessed since they were in diapers.

How can educators encourage students to learn in a world where most students have almost an emotional connection to their personal devices? How can we reprogram bad habits (both inside and outside of the classroom) into good ones? How can classroom content be disseminated so that it is more accessible and interative? I made an honest attempt here to answer these questinos by reorganizing my course material so that it is more accessible to my students. Seemless, responsive, and informative course design.
